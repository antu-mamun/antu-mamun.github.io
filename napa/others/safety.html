Safety and Efficacy

Napa can be given in – High blood pressure, Kidney Disease, Pregnancy & Lactation, Aspirin Heart Therapy, Stomach Conditions.




Aspirin Heart Therapy

    Paracetamol Does not compromise the cardioprotective benefits of Aspirin.


    Aspirin binds to platelet COX-1 to inhibit platelet aggregation and to protect against myocardial infarction. Ibuprofen binds to the same area of the COX-1 enzyme. When a patient takes ibuprofen prior to taking aspirin, aspirin is blocked from reaching its binding sites.
    Plus, adding an NSAID to aspirin can increase the risk of stomach bleeding.
    Thus taking Ibuprofen will compromise the cardio protective benefits of Aspirin heart therapy. But Paracetamol does not interfere with Aspirin’s cardio protective mechanism and thus does not compromise cardiac functions of patients on Aspirin Heart Therapy.


    The American Heart Association endorsed Paracetamol for patients with known cardiovascular disease or risk factors for ischemic heart disease.


    Napa,

    A safer choice for Cardiac patients.

High Blood Pressure

    NAPA® may be an appropriate pain relief option for you if you have high blood pressure.

    There are a lots of patients those who need to take both NSAIDs and Antihypertensive drugs. This overlap is significant, because NSAIDs limit the effectiveness of antihypertensive drugs.
    Overviews of clinical trial data indicate that the blood pressure of patients with controlled hypertension can be raised during concurrent treatment with NSAIDs, which can produce a significant increase in subsequent stroke, end-stage renal disease, or congestive heart failure. Unless an NSAID is deemed absolutely necessary, the clinician may consider alternative treatment i.e. Paracetamol.

    Because-

  1. Paracetamol has not been observed to increase blood pressure.

  2. Paracetamol has not been reported to have interactions with antihypertensive agents.

Kidney

    The National Kidney Foundation recommends acetaminophen, the active ingredient in NAPA®, as the pain reliever of choice for occasional use in patients that have underlying kidney disease. 

    In kidneys with some degree of dysfunction, NSAIDs’ COX inhibition can result in reduced renal perfusion (COX-1 effect) and altered tubular Sodium and water handling (COX- effect), potentially further compromising renal function and promoting fluid retention. Paracetamol does not compromise renal function in patients with existing kidney dysfunction.

    Clinical data have established that Paracetamol in recommended doses is not Nephrotoxic. Clinical trials measuring renal function in patients taking at least 1000 mg of Paracetamol daily for at least 1 year revealed that, no evidence of clinically significant renal impairment.

    Wise Use Painkiller Practices

    There are a few things you can do to ensure that you are using analgesic medicines properly.

  1. Always consult your doctor before using a painkiller. Pain is the body’s way of letting you know there is something wrong, masking the pain can make proper diagnosis more difficult.
  2. Follow package directions on OTC painkillers. Don’t take for more than 10 days and consult your doctor if pain continues.
  3. Avoid painkillers that include mixed analgesics such as ibuprofen, caffeine, and acetaminophen. The mixed painkillers are more likely to cause side-effects for kidney patients.
  4. Don’t take painkillers with alcohol.
  5. Be certain that your doctor knows ALL of the medicines you take, including OTC analgesics.

Older Adults

    The American Geriatric Society recommends acetaminophen, the medicine in NAPA®, as an important option for relieving mild-to-moderate musculoskeletal pain.
    Senior citizens require EXTRA care for pain management because they:

  1. Often suffer from multiple problems
  2. Have higher incidence of medication side effects
  3. Have higher potential for adverse effects

    4. Paracetamol, the active ingredient in Napa®, may be the right choice for older patients.
    Because Paracetamol is well established as an important pain relief option for people.

  4. With a history of stomach problems
  5. On Aspirin Heart Therapy
  6. With Kidney disease
  7. With High blood pressure

    8. NSAIDs on the other hand may:

  8. Irritate the stomach
  9. Reduces effectiveness of Aspirin Heart Therapy
  10. Increase abnormalities in kidney disease patients.
  11. Worsen conditions of patients with high blood pressure.

    12. Additionally, NSAIDs have a clearly established mechanism of interaction between certain high blood pressure treatments, such as diuretics, and can reduce the effectiveness of previously prescribed high blood pressure treatments.
    Similar effects have not been established for acetaminophen, the active ingredient in TYLENOL®.


    REMEMBER,

    Always talk to your doctor if you have a health condition or are taking prescription medicines before taking any pain reliever. If your pain hasn't subsided or gets worse after 10 days of use, you should stop taking your pain reliever and consult a physician.

Pregnancy & Lactation

    Paracetamol does not cause harm in pregnancy and Lactation

    It Can be given in any trimester of pregnancy.

    Acetaminophen is the safest medicine as analgesics for nociceptive pain and antipyretics in childhood and pregnancy. There is no alternative medication of acetaminophen. Acetaminophen should be used at the lowest effective dosage and for the shortest time.

    The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) classifies acetaminophen as compatible with breast-feeding. Acetaminophen is excreted in breast milk in low concentrations (0 .1% to 1 .85% of the ingested maternal dose). Accordingly, maternal ingestion of acetaminophen in labeled doses does not present a risk to the nursing infant. These studies have established that even the baby would receive less than 2% of the maternal dose.

    Napa,

    A safer choice for Pregnancy and lactation.

Safety Concern

  1. This product contains paracetamol; do not take it with other medicines that also contain paracetamol. Paracetamol is contained in many medicines to treat pain, fever, symptoms of cold and flu, and sleep medicines.
  2. If you have liver or kidney disease, talk to your doctor before taking paracetamol.
  3. Do not take more than the recommended dose, as this may be harmful, including serious harm to your liver.
